A web designer typically begins their process with a consultation to understand the client's needs, goals, and target audience. They then conduct research to analyze competitors and trends, which informs their design approach. The designer creates wireframes to outline the website's layout and flow, followed by developing the visual elements such as color schemes and typography using design software. Post-launch, they may provide ongoing maintenance, updating content and optimizing performance based on user behavior, all while combining creativity with technical skills to create visually appealing and user-friendly websites.
